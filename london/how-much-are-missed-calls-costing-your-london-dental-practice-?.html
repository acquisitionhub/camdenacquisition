How Much Are Missed Calls Really Costing Your London Dental Practice?

Most practices assume it's not that many. The reality is usually a shock — and the fix is simpler than you'd think.

By the Patient Acquisition Hub team · Updated April 2026 · 6 min read

← Back to Patient Acquisition Hub

If you asked most dental practice managers how many calls they miss in a week, they'd probably say "not many." In reality, when we look at the data from London practices we work with, the typical answer is around five missed calls per week — sometimes more.

At first that doesn't sound catastrophic. But let's look at what that actually means for your practice.

That's a conservative estimate. For practices with higher treatment values — implants, Invisalign, full mouth rehabilitation — the number can be significantly higher. And that's just one month. Over a year, you're potentially looking at £70,000+ in lost revenue from calls that nobody answered.

Why London Practices Miss So Many Calls

This isn't a staffing problem — it's a structural one. London dental receptions are genuinely busy. Here's when most calls get missed:

  • During treatments: The receptionist is assisting or the room is being cleared.
  • During lunch: Reduced cover means calls pile up.
  • First thing in the morning: High call volume while the team is getting set up.
  • After 5:30pm: Reception has closed, but patients are calling when they finish work.
  • Weekends: Many practices are closed, but patients are available.

The evening and weekend problem is particularly significant in London, where many patients are commuters with long working hours. They can't call at 11am on a Tuesday — they call at 7pm on a Thursday or on Saturday morning. And if nobody answers, they move on to the next practice on Google.

What Happens When a Patient Gets No Answer?

Here's the uncomfortable truth about missed calls: patients rarely call back. Research on consumer behaviour consistently shows that if someone calls a business and gets no answer, fewer than 30% will try again. The rest — the majority — simply move on.

In a competitive market like London, with dozens of nearby alternatives just a Google search away, those patients aren't going to wait around. They'll call the next practice on the list, get an answer, and book. You've lost them without ever knowing they called.

The patient's perspective: They searched "dentist near me," saw your practice, clicked your number, and called. That's high intent — they wanted to book with you. All they needed was someone to answer.

The Simple Fix: Instant SMS Recovery

The good news is that this problem is almost entirely solvable, and the solution doesn't require extra staff, a new phone system, or any changes to how your reception works.

Missed call recovery works like this: the moment a call goes unanswered, an automated SMS is sent to the caller — within 60 seconds — with a friendly message from your practice and a direct link to your online booking page. The patient gets a response immediately, and most of them book right then, even at 10pm.

The SMS feels personal because it's branded with your practice name and written in a warm, human tone. Patients respond well to it — better, often, than a call back the next morning when they're in a meeting and can't talk.
