Frequently Asked Questions — GBP Optimisation for Dentists

Why does GBP optimisation matter so much for dental practices?
GBP signals account for 32% of your Google Maps Pack ranking — the single biggest factor. For most dental practices, the Map Pack is where the majority of new patient enquiries originate. An under-optimised GBP means you're effectively invisible for searches where you should be the obvious local result.
What is profile lock and why does a dental practice need it?
Google occasionally suggests edits to business profiles — changes to opening hours, categories, or other details — and sometimes applies them automatically. Competitors can also suggest edits to your profile. Profile lock monitors your GBP 24/7 and alerts us immediately if any unauthorised changes are made, so we can revert them before they affect your ranking.
How does dental practice competitor analysis help my ranking?
Knowing what the top-ranked NW1 and NW3 practices have in their GBP gives you a precise roadmap. If a competitor has 12 GBP posts in the last 90 days and you have 2, that's a specific, fixable gap. If they have 40 photos and you have 8, that's actionable. Competitor analysis removes the guesswork and replaces it with specific targets.
How quickly does GBP optimisation affect my ranking?
GBP optimisation typically produces the fastest visible changes of any local SEO work — often within 1 to 2 weeks for category and service-related improvements. Full Map Pack ranking improvements for competitive NW1 and NW3 searches usually become visible within 4 to 8 weeks as the optimised profile accumulates new signals.
